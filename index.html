How to become a website developer


With the surge of online brands and services, now might be a better time than ever to start learning web design. But if you’re on the fence about whether to make this your full-time gig, then you’ll want to take the time to understand what you’re getting yourself into.

4 Steps to becoming a website developer

  • Get a plan
  • Learn basic languages
  • Practice coding

Below the above points are well explained. Keep reading!

- Get a plan:

To stand out in a crowded market, you’ll want to know your “who” and “why.” In other words, it’s a good idea to narrow down your focus and decide on the types of clients you’d like to take on.

- Learn basic languages:

When you look at a site on the web, the three most important things that make up what you see on the web are HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. So these are the first three things to learn as the foundation for becoming a front-end developer.

- Practice Coding:

There is a popular idiom that says “Practice makes perfect”. This means that you'll become better at something if you do it often.
